# Refund Policy

Last updated: 18 July 2026

If we change this policy, we publish the new version here at least 10 days before it takes effect. A change never applies backwards: money already on your balance stays covered by the version of this policy that was in force when you topped up.

This page explains when you get your money back for Lex, how to ask, and what happens next. It is part of our Terms and Conditions, which you can read at https://lex-me.club/terms/.

What you are buying

Lex is a Chrome extension. It is an English teacher that works on a YouTube video you choose — it gives you readable subtitles for that video and lets you talk to the teacher by text or voice. It works only on YouTube.

Installing Lex and creating an account are free. To use the teacher you top up a prepaid balance in your account, in US dollars. The balance is spent as you use the service.

There is no subscription and no automatic renewal. A payment happens only when you choose to top up.

System requirements: Google Chrome (Lex is a Manifest V3 extension), an internet connection, a Lex account, and a positive balance to use the teacher. Talking to the teacher by voice also needs a working microphone and your permission for Chrome to use it.

When we give money back

The rule is simple:

  • Unspent balance — refundable. Ask us and we send back what is left of what you paid in.
  • Spent balance — not refundable. Balance is spent as you use Lex: messages to the teacher, voice, preparing the subtitles for a video, looking a word up. Whatever has already been used cannot be returned.
  • Free credit — not refundable. If part of your balance is credit we added ourselves, that part is not money you paid in and cannot be paid out.

If you have spent part of a top-up, we refund the remainder.

This does not affect your statutory rights. You benefit from the legal guarantee of conformity: if the service did not work as described — something that failed, or a charge for something you did not receive — tell us and we will put it right or refund that part of the balance, whether or not it counts as spent.

We do not deduct anything from the refund ourselves. If your card or payment account is in a currency other than US dollars, the amount that reaches you depends on your bank's conversion rate at the time of the refund, which may differ from the rate applied when you paid.

How to ask for a refund

Write to nikolaev.gd@gmail.com from the email address on your Lex account, and say you want your unspent balance refunded. If you prefer to arrange it by phone, call +373 60 220 120.

That is all we need. You do not have to give a reason.

Support working hours: Monday to Friday, 09:00–18:00 (Chișinău time).

What happens next

  1. We confirm that we received your request.
  2. We check how much unspent balance is on your account at the moment we process your request. Anything spent between your email and that moment counts as spent, so we suggest you stop using Lex once you have asked.
  3. We deduct that amount from your balance and the refund is issued back to you through the payment provider that processed your payment.

We answer refund requests and send the money within 14 days of receiving them. The money goes back by the same method you used to pay — to the same card or the same payment account. We cannot send it anywhere else. After the refund is sent, the time it takes to appear on your statement is set by the payment provider and your own bank: how quickly it then appears on your statement is up to your bank — usually a few business days.

How you pay, and how the refund travels back

Card details are entered on the secure page of our payment provider. They go to the provider, not to us — we never see or store your card number, expiry date or security code.

Card payments are authenticated under 3-D Secure. Depending on your bank and on the payment, your bank may ask you to confirm it — for example with a one-time code — or it may authenticate it in the background without asking you anything.

If your card is in a currency other than the currency of the payment, your bank applies its own conversion terms. That is between you and your bank; we cannot influence it.

Your statutory right to withdraw (14 days)

Under the Civil Code of the Republic of Moldova, a consumer who buys at a distance has the right to withdraw from the contract (art. 1059), and the withdrawal period is 14 days (art. 1060 para. (1)). For a service the 14 days run from the day after the contract is concluded; for digital content that is not supplied on a physical medium they run from the day the contract is concluded.

How this works with Lex. Your top-up is delivered immediately: the balance appears in your account as soon as the payment is confirmed. Each time you then use Lex, digital content is supplied to you without a physical medium. Under art. 1065 para. (1) letter m), the right of withdrawal falls away for content that has already been supplied, where the supply began with your prior express consent and with your confirmation that you lose the withdrawal right at that moment. It does not fall away for balance you have not spent.

Before you confirm a top-up we ask you to agree, in a separate step that is never pre-ticked, that we start immediately and that you accept losing the right of withdrawal for the part of the balance you actually use. We never tick that box for you and never infer your agreement from a pre-selected option (art. 1065 para. (2)).

In practice this does not cost you anything, because our own policy is more generous than the statutory minimum: whatever is left unspent on your balance comes back on request, before or after the 14 days, whether or not you gave that consent.

How to withdraw. Tell us by any clear statement — an email to nikolaev.gd@gmail.com is enough. You may also use the standard withdrawal form set out in Annex no. 6 to Law no. 1125/2002; ask us and we will email it to you.

If we failed to give you the information about the right of withdrawal required by art. 1015 para. (1) letter h), the withdrawal period expires 12 months after the end of the initial 14 days. If we give you that information at any point within those 12 months, the period expires 14 days after you receive it (art. 1061).
