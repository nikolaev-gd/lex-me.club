### What Lex is

Lex is a Google Chrome browser extension. It is an English teacher that works on a YouTube video you choose yourself.

The teacher is software, not a person. Your questions, the subtitles of the video and — in voice mode — your speech are processed by third-party AI models, and those models produce the answers. Like any AI, the teacher can be wrong or incomplete. Lex is a learning aid: it is not certified tuition and it does not lead to any qualification.

Inside the extension you get:

- readable subtitles for the video you are watching — Lex takes the captions YouTube provides for that video and turns them into punctuated, clickable sentences; if the video has no captions, Lex processes its audio in order to write the speech out as text;
- a chat with the teacher about that video — by text or by voice;
- clicking a word in the subtitles, which is a shortcut for asking the teacher about that word.

The lesson works **only on YouTube**: the Lex subtitles, the video chat and the click-a-word shortcut run on YouTube watch pages and on Shorts, and nowhere else. The chat window can also be opened from the toolbar icon over whatever other page you happen to have open, so that you can ask the teacher a question without leaving it — but outside YouTube there is no video for the teacher to work with.

Lex is not a video downloader. It gives you no copy of any video and no video or audio file you can keep. It works with the YouTube page you already have open.
